Adam Arkin Biography

Adam  Arkin is an American actor and director who was born on 19 August 1956 in Brooklyn, New York, United States. He has been nominated for various awards, including a Tony as well as three primetime Emmys, four SAG Awards, and a DGA Award.

Adam Career

Adam began his career in acting in 1975 where he guest-starred in the award-winning television show Happy Days in episode 35, season 2.  Adam’s first starring role in television was as Lenny Markowitz, the central character in the 1977 series Busting Loose. Since then he has appeared in numerous television series such as The Twilight Zone (1986), Northern Exposure (CBS, 1990–1995), and  A Year in the Life (1988)

He also joined other movies and Television shows such as; Sons of Anarchy (2008 – 2014), Full Moon High (1987), Chu Chu, and the Philly Flash (1981), and Hitch (2005). He has been nominated for various awards, including a Tony as well as three primetime Emmys, four SAG Awards, and a DGA Award.
