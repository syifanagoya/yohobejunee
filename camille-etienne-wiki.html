Camille Etienne Career

At an early age, Camille Étienne started participating in activism. She was appointed the On est Prêt collective's spokesman in 2018. She and Cyril Dion carried a platform to encourage storytellers to create original stories in 2019 at the Cannes Film Festival. The ecological issue is the main topic of interest for Camille Étienne. She rose to the position of president of Amnesty International's student chapter at Sciences Po.

She founded the Avant l'orage group while confined. Camille Étienne accepted an invitation to serve as editor-in-chief of the Socialter 28 magazine's special issue titled "Ecology or Death" in December 2021. Camille Étienne is also devoted to opposing projects that produce a lot of greenhouse gas emissions, particularly seabed mining, and the EACOP, the biggest heated oil pipeline in the world that TotalEnergies is planning to build in East Africa.
